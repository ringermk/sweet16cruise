Safety: Smart + Fun

We are here to have an amazing trip — not to be anxious. So this is the “be chill, be aware” guide: quick rules, easy habits, and what to do if something feels off.

Goal: fun + safe Vibe: confident Rule: trust your gut

TL;DR (read this even if you read nothing else)

Stay together (or use the buddy rule)
If you leave the ship, nobody wanders alone. Two people minimum. Always.
Keep your phone + a little cash secured
Crossbody bag, front pocket, hand on it in crowds. Back pockets are a “donate to strangers” program.
If it feels weird, it is weird
You never have to be polite to someone making you uncomfortable. Walk away. Find a crew member. Call us.
If we get separated
Pick a meet-up spot before splitting up. If someone gets separated: stop moving, step to the side, and call or text. If there’s no response in 5 minutes, go straight to the meet-up spot.

Falling overboard: the real talk

This is rare — but it’s also the kind of rare that’s not negotiable, so we respect the ocean.

🌊

How often?

Research frequently cited by major outlets estimates ~21 people per year go overboard (cruise ships + ferries) since 2010.

🛟

Survival rate

One widely cited analysis reports fewer than 1 in 5 are rescued alive.

📌

Another estimate

A U.S. congressional hearing summary cited a rescue-alive rate of about 16.7% for overboard cases.

How we stay safe on deck
No sitting or standing on railings. No climbing. No leaning out for photos. Balconies are for relaxing — not for anything that would make a mom panic.

On the ship

Balconies + railings: treat them like lava
No sitting/standing on railings. No climbing. No leaning over for photos. Ever.
Elevators + stairs
Hold the handrail on stairs (ships move). Watch for wet floors near pools. Flip-flops are adorable. Also slippery.
Drink safety
Don’t accept open drinks from strangers. Don’t leave your drink unattended. If it tastes “off,” toss it.
Cabin rules
Cabin door locked. Don’t let random people into your room. If housekeeping is there, great — otherwise: nope.
If someone makes you uncomfortable
Move toward a crew member or a busy area. Call or text us. You do not owe anyone conversation, attention, or politeness.

In port / off the ship

Buddy rule (always)
No one walks alone. Two people minimum — even “just real quick.”
Be confidently boring
Don’t flash cash. Don’t show passports. Don’t announce your plans out loud. Act like you’ve done this 100 times.
Keep your essentials secure
Crossbody bag worn in front. Phone on a lanyard or deep pocket. Wallet not in back pocket. Ever.
The “pressure sales” escape line
Practice: “No thank you.” (Repeat.) Keep walking. Don’t debate. Don’t explain.
Pickpocket-proof basics
Keep one hand on your bag in crowds. Don’t leave phones on tables or bags on chair backs. If someone bumps or distracts you, immediately check your stuff.

Taxi safety (and how not to get scammed)

Use official taxi lines
At ports, go to the clearly marked taxi area. Avoid random “I can take you cheaper” approaches.
Agree on the plan before you get in
Confirm: where you’re going + whether the price is per person or total + round-trip pickup time.
Cash rules
Small bills. Count it discreetly. Don’t pull out a huge wad of cash like you’re in a movie.
If anything feels sketchy
Don’t get in. Step back. Find another driver in the official line. Your safety is not a negotiation.
Common tourist traps
“Free” things that become not-free later. Surprise add-ons. People who won’t clearly answer pricing questions. If it’s vague, it’s a no.

Avoid surprise charges (on ship + on shore)

Ask: “Is this included?”
Before you say yes to anything: photos, tastings, upgrades, “special access,” whatever.
Watch for auto-adds
Some things come with service fees/gratuities. Read the receipt screen. Don’t rush-click “OK.”
Shore excursions
Know what’s included: transport, entry fees, gear, tips. If it’s vague, it’s a no.
Receipts = power
If something feels wrong, take a photo of the receipt and we’ll sort it out calmly.

If something goes wrong

Find a crew member
Crew are your safe adults on the ship. If you need help: go to them first.
Keep a screenshot of the ship card + cabin number
If your phone dies, you still need to know where “home” is and how to get there.
Ports: return early
We build a buffer. Because missing the ship would be… a plot twist we don’t need.
If something feels wrong
You’re allowed to be dramatic about safety. Call us even if you can’t explain why. The only wrong move is staying quiet and hoping it goes away.
