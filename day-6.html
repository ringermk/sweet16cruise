Day 6 - Costa Maya, Mexico

A short port day with big “wander, snack, relax” energy. This is a choose-your-own-adventure kind of morning.

Arrive: 7:00 AM Depart: 1:00 PM Back onboard: ~11:00 AM

The plan (in one sentence)

We keep this morning easy: either stay on the ship and enjoy the quiet, or step off for a relaxed wander around the port village — no rushing, no pressure.

Option A: Stay on the ship

This is one of the best mornings to stay onboard. Most people get off early, which means the ship is calm and uncrowded.

Why this is a great idea
Empty decks, easier breakfast seating, quiet pool time, and zero stress about getting back on time.
Things to do onboard
Sleep in a little, get breakfast without lines, enjoy the pool or hot tubs, explore parts of the ship we skipped earlier, or just sit with ocean views.

Option B: Step off the ship

Costa Maya is one of the easiest ports to explore without an excursion. The port village is walkable, colorful, and designed for cruising guests.

Free & easy things to do
Walk the port village, take photos by the colorful buildings, watch the dancers or musicians, and enjoy the open-air vibe.
Relaxed extras (budget-friendly)
Grab a drink, sit by the pool area near the port, or just people-watch and enjoy being off the ship without going far.

How this compares to other ports

Compared to Roatán
Unlike Roatán (where we planned snorkeling at West Bay), Costa Maya works best as a low-effort port. No taxis required, no long travel time, no tight schedules.
Compared to Cozumel
This day is shorter than Cozumel and less about beaches — think strolling, photos, snacks, and being back onboard early.
