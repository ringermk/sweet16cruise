Day 3 - Hello, Cozumel

We dock early, grab our sea-day legs, and decide our vibe: explore the island… or stay onboard and enjoy the ship while it’s extra chill.

Arrive: 7:30 AM Depart: 4:30 PM All aboard: 2:30 PM

Non-negotiable timing

If anyone leaves the ship, they must be back on board by 2:30 PM. That’s our “no stress, no sprinting” cutoff.

Also: double-check we’re using ship time (not phone time). Phones can be sneaky in port.

What to bring off-ship

SeaPass key, ID, sunscreen, water, cash/tip money, and comfy shoes.

And: don’t forget your SeaPass/room key is the golden ticket for basically everything. (If you’re wearing it on a lanyard, you’re already winning.)

Simple plan for the day

Morning
Breakfast → decide: “off-ship adventure” or “ship day deluxe.”
Midday
If exploring: build in buffer time to get back early. If staying: enjoy shorter lines and quieter decks.
2:30 PM
All aboard (seriously). After that, it’s ship mode, snacks, and watch the world sail away.
4:30 PM
People watch for stragglers coming back. Watch the drama unfold as cruisers come back late risking being stuck in Cozumel.

Budget-friendly things to do if you leave the ship

These are “low-cost, low-stress” ideas (confidence: medium-high — options vary depending on the port area and what’s open that day).

Walk the waterfront + the big letters photo
Stay close, take pics, people-watch, find shade, and soak in the “we’re in Mexico” vibe.
Souvenir mission with a spending cap
Each person gets a limit (like $20). Goal: find one thing that feels “Cozumel” without going broke.
Beach day… the simple version
Pick a nearby beach spot where you can just chill, swim, and snack. Keep it simple and stay mindful of return time.
Local lunch & a cold drink
Find a casual spot, split food, and hydrate like a champion. Bonus if it comes with ocean breeze.
DIY photo scavenger list (free)
Snap: a colorful mural, a palm-tree shot, something blue, something silly, and a “we did it” selfie.
Port day safety vibe (tap)

Stick together, keep valuables minimal, don’t wander too far, and set an alarm for “head back to the ship.” The goal is fun, not stress.

Budget-friendly things to do if you stay on the ship

This is the “ship is quieter, we are thriving” option. (confidence: high)

Pool deck without the sea-day chaos
Grab a lounger, sun time, dip in the pool — and you don’t have to fight anyone for a spot.
Photo walk around the ship
Find the best ocean views, best “fancy staircase” shot, best mirror, best cozy corner.
Ship schedule browsing
In the afternoon or evening, we check the daily schedule and pick tomorrow’s: show, class, trivia, game show, whatever looks fun.
Snack crawl
Split dessert, try a new snack, rate everything 1–10. Absolutely scientific.
Quiet lounge + book / music
Find a calm spot with an ocean view and pretend we’re the main character in a travel montage.

Activity suggestions

Choose 1 “active thing” + 1 “chill thing” and you’ve basically nailed the day.

Active
Off-ship walking/photo mission OR onboard pool/waterslide time.
Chill
Coffee + ocean view OR lounge time + ship schedule planning for tomorrow.
Evening reset
Back in the room: quick shower, comfy clothes, and we pick tomorrow’s “must-do” from the schedule.
