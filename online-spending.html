How the cruise card works (the friendly truth)

Everyone gets a card (or sometimes a wearable key) that’s linked to the stateroom account. It’s used to open the room, get on/off the ship, and — if charging is enabled — pay for stuff onboard.

What it always does
Room access • Ship ID • Getting on/off at ports • Checking into activities
What it might do (if charging is enabled)
Purchases onboard • Upgrades • Add-ons • “Tap and done” spending

What can be charged with the card

Most surprise charges come from fun little stuff that adds up fast. Here are the usual suspects.

🍹 Drinks & treats
Specialty coffees, mocktails, smoothies, bottled water, premium desserts, certain room service items.
🎮 Arcade & attractions
Arcade games, specialty activities (varies by ship), anything that feels like “just a quick tap.”
🛍️ Shops
Souvenirs, ship hoodies, candy, little “I NEEDED this” items. (Teen logic is undefeated.)
📸 Photos
Professional photos and photo packages. They’re cute. They’re also sneaky expensive.
📶 Internet / communication upgrades
Wi-Fi packages or upgrades. This is another common “oops” category.

How we prevent unauthorized purchases

Mom-friendly: this is easy and you’re not the first parent to ask. Teen-friendly: this isn’t punishment — it’s just guardrails.

Option A: Full charging block (recommended)
Ask Guest Services to disable onboard charging for Jaekin’s card. Her card still works for the room + getting on/off the ship. Purchases require an adult to do the transaction.
Option B: “Fun money” only
Add a small onboard credit amount (example: $40–$75 for the whole cruise) and keep everything else blocked. When it’s gone, it’s gone — no drama, no surprise bill.
Option C: Set a spending limit (ask if available)
Sometimes Guest Services can set a cap. If they can’t on this sailing, Options A + B accomplish the same goal.

Step-by-step: what to do on embarkation day

1) Decide the plan before we board
Are we doing full block, or block + fun money? (My vote: block + a small fun budget.)
2) Go to Guest Services
Ask: “Can you disable onboard charging for the minor’s card?” If we want fun money: “And can we add $___ onboard credit for her to use?”
3) Do a tiny test
After it’s set up, we can confirm it works: Jaekin tries to charge a $0 or low-cost item (or we ask Guest Services to verify). Better to find out now than at 11pm with a line behind us.
4) Make the “ask first” rule easy
Jaekin can text/call (or find us) before buying something. If it’s reasonable, we do it together. It’s not “no,” it’s “check first.”

Our mini “money agreement” (teen-friendly)

This is the part where everyone stays happy. Clear rules = more freedom.

✅ Approved stuff
One treat drink a day (if we choose) • Snacks • One souvenir • Arcade within fun money • Anything we say “yes” to
🚫 Not without asking
Wi-Fi upgrades • Photo packages • Shopping sprees • “It said it was included” mysteries
Best question on a cruise
“Is this included?” If the answer isn’t a clear “yes,” we pause and check.

Daily habit: quick account check

This takes 2 minutes and prevents 90% of stress.

Check once per day
Pick a time: after breakfast or after dinner. Look at the onboard account totals.
Receipts = power
If something seems wrong, take a photo of the receipt/screen (if possible) and go to Guest Services calmly.
If an “oops” happens
It’s not a crisis. We handle it early, politely, and it’s usually fixable.

Quick FAQ

Will blocking charging stop her from getting on/off the ship?
No — her card still works as an ID and for boarding/port scans.
Can she still do teen activities?
Yes. The restriction is about spending, not access to teen spaces and ship activities.
What if she wants to buy something?
Easy: she asks an adult, and we do the purchase together on an adult card (or we add more fun money if we choose).

Final vibe check

This is not about trust. It’s about cruise systems being a little too easy. Guardrails keep it fun for everyone — and Mom gets to actually relax. ❤️

Our default plan
Block charging + choose a small fun money amount + quick daily account check. Done. Vacation protected.
